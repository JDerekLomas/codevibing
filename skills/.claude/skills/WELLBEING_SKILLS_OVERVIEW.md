# Wellbeing Design Skills Overview

A comprehensive skill set for designing products and interfaces that support human flourishing, grounded in Pieter Desmet's 13 fundamental human needs framework, rigorous psychometrics, research-through-design, and leading methodologies from TU Delft and Tongji University.

## Your Skills

### 1. **Wellbeing Design with Desmet's 13 Needs**
**Directory**: `wellbeing-design-desmet/`

The core framework for understanding and designing for human wellbeing. Based on Pieter Desmet's comprehensive model of 13 fundamental needs that, when satisfied, lead to flourishing.

**Use this skill when**:
- Designing new features or products
- Evaluating existing interfaces for wellbeing impact
- Creating design checklists and audit criteria
- Defining success metrics beyond engagement
- Addressing specific wellbeing dimensions (autonomy, purpose, connection, etc.)

**Key sections**:
- The 13 needs explained (psychological, hedonic, eudaimonic, social, transcendent)
- Design patterns for each need
- Integration with psychology research
- Practical checklist for review

---

### 2. **Wellbeing Psychometrics & Measurement**
**Directory**: `wellbeing-psychometrics/`

Validated instruments and measurement approaches for assessing wellbeing outcomes. Combines understanding of psychometric principles with accessible descriptions of evidence-based scales.

**Use this skill when**:
- Designing research studies to measure wellbeing impact
- Selecting measurement instruments for your context
- Interpreting wellbeing data and effect sizes
- Creating surveys or feedback mechanisms
- Integrating quantitative validation with design work

**Key sections**:
- Psychometric principles (validity, reliability, model fit)
- Validated multidimensional instruments (PERMA-Profiler, PWI-8, PWB)
- Emotional and flourishing-specific scales
- Real-time and experience sampling methods
- Measurement design best practices
- Interpretation guidance and common pitfalls

### 3. **Qualitative Research-Through-Design**
**Directory**: `research-through-design/`

Rigorous qualitative methodology treating design artifacts as research instruments. Combines iterative prototyping, participant co-creation, and systematic analysis to investigate wellbeing dimensions.

**Use this skill when**:
- Conducting user research for wellbeing-centered design
- Creating prototypes to investigate design possibilities
- Analyzing qualitative data from user sessions
- Establishing design principles from research findings
- Engaging participants as co-researchers

**Key sections**:
- RtD methodology and core principles
- Three-phase process (framing, exploration, refinement)
- Data collection methods (observation, interview, annotation)
- Qualitative analysis and thematic coding
- Wellbeing-specific considerations (emotional safety, authenticity)
- Integration with psychometrics

## How the Skills Work Together

```
START: You're designing a wellbeing-focused product

1. FRAME THE CHALLENGE
   └─> Use Design Methods (TU Delft & Tongji)
       Stakeholder mapping, value identification, experience framing

2. UNDERSTAND WELLBEING DIMENSIONS
   └─> Use Desmet's 13 Needs
       What dimensions matter for your users and context?

3. CONDUCT RESEARCH
   └─> Use Research-Through-Design
       Participant co-creation, iterative prototyping, lived experience

4. DESIGN WITH INTENTION
   └─> Use Desmet's 13 Needs + Design Methods
       Apply patterns, consider values, create emotionally resonant experiences

5. MEASURE IMPACT
   └─> Use Psychometrics & Evaluation Framework
       Select validated instruments, analyze results, interpret findings

6. REFINE & EVOLVE
   └─> Loop back through earlier skills
       Monitor longitudinally, engage communities, adapt based on learning
```

## Core Framework: Desmet's 13 Needs at a Glance

**Core Psychological Needs** (essential for wellbeing):
1. **Autonomy** - Freedom, choice, self-direction
2. **Competence** - Mastery, effectiveness, capability
3. **Relatedness** - Connection, belonging, meaningful relationships

**Hedonic Needs** (emotional pleasure):
4. **Pleasure** - Positive sensory/emotional experiences
5. **Comfort** - Ease, reduction of discomfort
6. **Stimulation** - Novelty, challenge, engagement

**Eudaimonic Needs** (meaningful living):
7. **Self-expression** - Authentic representation
8. **Self-development** - Growth, learning, skill
9. **Purpose** - Meaningful contribution, values alignment

**Social & Transcendent Needs**:
10. **Benevolence** - Helping others, contribution
11. **Justice** - Fairness, equity, moral rectitude
12. **Self-care** - Self-respect, healthy boundaries
13. **Spiritual connection** - Connection to something larger

## Recommended Learning Path

If you're new to wellbeing design:

1. **Start**: Desmet's 13 Needs (understand the framework)
2. **Deepen**: Design Methods (learn systematic approaches)
3. **Practice**: Research-Through-Design (conduct real studies)
4. **Measure**: Psychometrics (quantify and validate)
5. **Evaluate**: Evaluation Framework (assess impact)

If you already practice design research:

1. **Start**: Research-Through-Design (recognize your methods in here)
2. **Extend**: Desmet's 13 Needs (new lens for analysis)
3. **Enhance**: Psychometrics (add rigor to measurement)
4. **Integrate**: Design Methods (systematic frameworks)
5. **Master**: Evaluation Framework (comprehensive evaluation)

## Key Principles Across All Skills

- **Rigorous**: Grounded in research, peer-reviewed frameworks, validated instruments
- **Human-centered**: Users/participants as co-creators, not just subjects
- **Pluralistic**: Acknowledge multiple values; make trade-offs transparent
- **Ethical**: Prioritize participant wellbeing over business metrics
- **Practical**: Actionable methods, real-world examples, decision frameworks
- **Adaptive**: Support evolution and learning, not one-time fixes
- **Equitable**: Ensure all stakeholders have voice, especially the vulnerable
